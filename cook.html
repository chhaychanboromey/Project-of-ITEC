How to make Num Ansom?

Num Ansom is a traditional Cambodian dessert, and it refers to a type of sticky rice cake that is wrapped in banana leaves and typically enjoyed during special occasions, celebrations, and festivals. The name "Num Ansom" can be translated to mean "sticky rice cake wrapped in banana leaves."

We have known about the history of Num Ansom,so let us tell you about the ingredient to make Num Ansom in Cambodia.we need to have a Clutinous Rice that is the main component of Num Ansom provide a stcky and chewy texture,Mung Beans often included in the filling,Grated Coconut to adds a rich and cocontty flavor to the filling,Palm sugar to make it sweet,and Banana leaves to wrap the sticky rice cake imparting a distinct aroma during the steaming process.

After we have finish prepare,we steam a glutinous rice and soaked mung beans seperately until they are both fully cooked and have a sticky consistency.We mix it togetehr,wrap it with banana leaf and then arrange in a steamer and steam for about 45 minutes to 1 hour or until the rice is fully cooked and has a sticky consistency.

Num Ansom is considered a symbol of unity and familial bonds, often prepared and enjoyed during significant events such as Pchum Ben (Ancestors' Day) and Khmer New Year. The unique combination of ingredients and the use of banana leaves contribute to its distinctive taste and cultural significance in Cambodian cuisine.
