Technology in Cambodia has improve day by day.

AAccording to the latest data from the Data Reportal, there were 11.37 million internet users in Cambodia at the start of 2023, when internet penetration stood at 67.5 percent. Cambodia was home to 10.95 million social media users in January 2023, equating to 65 percent of the total population. The country also had 22 million mobile connections in January 2023, which means that there were more mobile connections than people living in Cambodia. The growth of digital technology has enabled Cambodians to access information, education, health, entertainment, and other services online. It has also created a vibrant ecosystem for technology startups, which are innovating and solving problems in various sectors such as agriculture, education, health, and climate change.

A recent report by the Asian Development Bank assessed the current ecosystem for tech-based startups in Cambodia, focusing on these four key sectors (agriculture, education, health, and climate change). It found that there were more than 100 active tech startups in Cambodia as of June 2022, with most of them operating in Phnom Penh, the capital city. The report also identified some of the challenges facing tech startups in Cambodia, such as limited access to capital, talent, infrastructure, and market. It provided recommendations to overcome these challenges, such as enhancing the governance structure of the science, technology and innovation (STI) system, developing the national STI workforce, strengthening research capacity and quality, increasing collaboration and linkages between different actors, and fostering an enabling environment for innovation.

The Cambodian government has also recognized the importance of STI for the country’s development and recovery from the COVID-19 pandemic. In 2019, the National Science, Technology and Innovation Policy 2020–2030 was adopted to build national capabilities in STI and to strengthen innovation capacity to respond to the fundamental needs of the nation. In 2020, the Ministry of Industry, Science, Technology and Innovation (MISTI) was established to lead and coordinate STI initiatives that further develop national capacities in this area, support key stakeholders and create favourable framework conditions.
